Did You Know Steel? The Science Behind Magnetic Sorting

Magnetic separation revolutionized scrap metal recycling by making it more efficient and environmentally friendly.

Magnetic separation is one of the most fundamental processes in modern scrap metal recycling. But why is it so important? Let's dive into the science and benefits of properly sorting ferrous from non-ferrous metals.

What Makes Steel Magnetic?

Steel is magnetic because it contains iron, a ferromagnetic material. Ferromagnetic materials can be magnetized and have the ability to retain magnetism. This property makes it easy to separate steel from other metals using powerful electromagnets in recycling facilities.

The Environmental Impact

Proper sorting ensures metals are recycled through the right processes. Mixing copper with steel can reduce the overall quality of both recycled materials, potentially decreasing the demand and value in recycling markets.

Key Takeaway

Magnetic separation isn't just about efficiency—it's about maximizing environmental benefits and getting the best value for your scrap materials.

Why It Matters to You

At K&L Recycling, proper sorting means you'll get fair market value for all your ferrous metals. Our experienced team uses advanced sorting techniques to ensure maximum return on your investment.

Steel Cable Scrap: Current Market Values in East Texas

Understanding how global demand and local markets influence steel cable prices.

Steel cable scrap, also known as wire rope or cable scrap, is one of the most common ferrous materials we process at K&L Recycling. However, pricing can vary significantly based on several factors.

Current Market Conditions

As of October 2024, steel cable scrap in the East Texas region is trading at $0.08 - $0.12 per pound, depending on cleanliness and size. Clean, uninsulated steel cable typically commands the highest prices.

Factors Affecting Price

  • Cleanliness: Cable with significant insulation or mixed materials sells at lower rates
  • Size: Larger diameter cables (1" or thicker) often get better pricing due to demand from mills
  • Global Market: Steel pricing fluctuates with international demand and US export trends
  • Transportation Costs: Heavy cables can be expensive to transport to processing facilities

Preparation Tips

For maximum value, remove as much non-ferrous material as possible before delivery. Cut cables into manageable lengths (no longer than 10 feet for easier handling).

Pro Tip: If your cable contains valuable copper braid or insulation, consider processing it as insulated wire instead of pure steel cable.

Separating Metals: Why Proper Sorting Matters

The environmental and economic benefits of proper metal separation in East Texas recycling.

At K&L Recycling, proper metal separation is the foundation of everything we do. It's not just about creating valuable products for our customers—it's about maximizing our environmental impact.

Energy Conservation Through Recycling

Recycling scrap metal requires significantly less energy than producing new metal from raw ore:

  • Steel: 75% less energy than mining and smelting
  • Copper: 85% energy savings
  • Aluminum: 95% energy reduction

Our East Texas Commitment

Serving the East Texas community since 1956, we've processed millions of tons of scrap metal while reducing our carbon footprint through efficient, certified recycling processes.

Community Impact

Every ton of metal recycled through proper separation saves enough energy to power an average East Texas home for 150 days.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I get the best price for my scrap metal?

Clean materials always fetch higher prices. Remove contaminants, separate ferrous from non-ferrous metals, and bring identified materials to our facility for expert grading.

What types of metal do you accept?

We accept ferrous metals (steels and irons), non-ferrous metals (copper, aluminum, brass), precious metals, and electronics. See our Materials Guide for complete details.

How does recycling help the environment?

Recycling conserves natural resources, reduces energy consumption, minimizes landfill waste, and significantly lowers CO2 emissions compared to mining new materials.
